Constellation Visual Studies

Five explorations for the Pal Constellation map on the Collection page. Inspired by Co-Star, Sanctuary, Stellarium, The Pattern, and TimePassages. Each study uses the same 10 pals and the same bestPal links so the differences are purely visual.

1 // Zodiac Wheel

Co-Star · Sanctuary

Pals occupy houses on a 10-wedge zodiac ring. Each pal gets its own house glyph and tribal sigil. The active pal's wedge brightens. Center holds a small sigil of the active pal. Tap a wedge to read its bestPal link and effect. Reads as a horoscope, plays as a roster.

2 // Night Sky Map

Stellarium · Sky Guide

A real-feeling star chart. Pals scatter across a deep nebula sky at varying magnitudes (size = luck). Asterism lines connect bestPals with the same drawing convention used for Orion or the Big Dipper. Lit pairs glow phosphor; dormant pairs stay white.

3 // Aspect Web

Co-Star birth chart · TimePassages

Pals sit on the rim at fixed degrees. Inside, aspect chords crisscross like a real natal chart. Color encodes relationship state: green = lit (trine), blue = adjacent (sextile), purple = tense (square), gold = completed loop (conjunction). Most data-rich of the five.

Lit Sextile Tense Loop

4 // Tarot Spread

Sanctuary · Chani · Labyrinthos

Each pal becomes a major-arcana card tinted to its color, with a Roman numeral, glyph, and name. Cards lay out as a horizontal fan; glowing thread arcs link bestPals overhead. Tap a card to flip it (active = raised). Most "physical" / collectible feeling.

5 // Orbital System

The Pattern · Stellarium · Kepler

Active pal = the sun. Other pals orbit at radii determined by luck (high luck = closer / brighter). BestPal links draw as comet arcs between orbits. Passive animation: planets drift around their orbit slowly. Reads as a living system rather than a static chart.
